International Speech Lexicon Project
Authors: Mark Hasegawa-Johnson and Margaret Fleck
Created 6/6/2007; last updated 7/17/2017

ISLEX is an on-again, off-again project whose goal is to provide feature-rich dictionaries for speech technology. The current English dictionary has 300,000 entries, culled from open-source dictionaries including CMUdict and Moby.

LICENSE: ISLEdict is distributed under the CC-BY 4.0 license. We try to make sure that all of our sources have license terms that are at least as permissive.

FEATURES: The dictionary includes the following information. Pronunciation is available for every word. Syllabification and lexical stress are available for many words. Part of speech and named entity tags (when appropriate) exist for most words. Morphology exists for only a few words.

  1. Part of speech is derived from the Brill part of speech tagger, from the SIL KTagger program, and from named entity files.
  2. Named entity tags are labeled with "nnp" or "nnps", followed by the named entity type. The following tags currently exist.
    • (nnp (surname|boyname|girlname) \d\.\d\d\d) specifies that the entry was logged as a surname, male given name, or female given name in the 1990 U.S. Census, or was listed as one of these name types in a relevant wikipedia entry, or (in the case of manual verification) elsewhere on the web. The number at the end of the entry specifies the percent frequency of the surname, male name, or female name in 1990 U.S. Census data, rounded to the nearest thousandth of one percent.
    • (nnp city) tags include cities with more than 100,000 inhabitants, U.S. state capitols, and other cities.
    • (nnp country) includes member states of the United Nations, and an assortment of other country-like entities (dependent protectorates, historical nations, etc.).
    • (nnp continent) and (nnp state) are used to mark continents and U.S. states.
    • (nnp place) is used for other places including rivers, mountains, planets and moons, and mythical places.
    • (nnp product) is used generically for registered trademarks, generic names of medicines, types of music, and names of philosophies (e.g., dixieland (nnp product)).
    • (nnp company) is a for-profit corporation; (nnp organization) is any non-country, non-company organization.
  3. Morphology: two types of morphological information are provided by the SIL KTagger program for the words it knows (with some extra labels provided by hand): 1. ( root:) specifies the root word 2. (morphology:...+...) specifies orthographic morpheme decomposition for words with known non-trivial decomposition.

SOURCES: We have processed and post-edited data from these sources, and we might therefore have introduced errors. Please blame the errors on us, not them.
