Countries and regions[edit]

This table lists 36 Bantu languages. Several languages from each zone, according to the classification Malcolm Guthrie, as follows:

Regions 1-12[edit]

  • Zone A20. Duala — Littoral (Douala) in Cameroon. 0,09 million speakers.
  • Zone A70. Fang — Cameroon, Gabon, Equatorial Guinea, R Congo. 0,858million speakers.
  • Zone B40. Punu — Nyanga, Ngounié in Gabon. 0, 132 million speakers.
  • Zone C30. Lingala — DR Congo, R Congo, CAR, Angola. 12,5 million speakers.
  • Zone D30. Kikumu (Komo) — Orientale Province, Tshopo District (Kisangani) in DR Congo. 0,4 million speakers.
  • Zone E20. Kikuyu — Central Province (Nairobi) in Kenya. 7,2 million speakers.
  • Zone F20. Sukuma — Northwest of Tanzania. 5,43 million speakers.
  • Zone G30. Zaramo — Pwani Region and Dar es Salaam in Tanzania. 0,657 million speakers.
  • Zone G40. Swahili — East Africa Official language in African Union. 46 million speakers.
  • Zone H10. Kongo — Angola, DR Congo, R Congo. 12 million speakers.
  • Zone H10. Kituba — Angola, DR Congo, R Congo. 5,4 million speakers.
  • Zone H20. Kimbundu — Luanda, Bengo, Malanje, Cuanza Norte in Angola. 3 million speakers.

Regions 13-24[edit]

  • Zone J10. Luganda — Central Region in Uganda. 6 million speakers.
  • Zone J30. Luhya — Western Province in Kenya; Eastern Region in Uganda. 4,6 million speakers.
  • Zone J60. Rwanda — Rwanda. 9,8 million speakers.
  • Zone J60. Rundi — Burundi. 8,8 million speakers.
  • Zone K10. Chokwe — Angola, DR Congo, Zambia. 1 million speakers.
  • Zone K30. Lunda — Zambia, DR Congo, Angola. 0.628 million speakers.
  • Zone L30. Tshiluba — Kasai-Occidental, Kasai-Oriental, Katanga in DR Congo. 9,5 million speakers.
  • Zone M40. Bemba — Copperbelt, Luapula, Northern in Zambia; Katanga in DR Congo. 3,6 million speakers.
  • Zone M60. Tonga (Malawi) — Southern in Zambia. 1,517 million speakers.
  • Zone N30. Chichewa — Malawi; Niassa, Tete, Zambezia in Mozambique; Eastern in Zambia. 11.5 million speakers.
  • Zone P20. Yao — Southern Region in Malawi; Niassa in Mozambique; Mtwara, Ruvuma in Tanzania. 2 million speakers.
  • Zone P30. Makhuwa — Nampula in Mozambique. 3,1 million speakers.

Regions 25-36[edit]

  • Zone R10. Umbundu — Benguela, Huambo, Bié in Angola. 4 million speakers.
  • Zone R20. Kwanyama — Cunene in Angola; Ovamboland in Namibia. 1,8 million speakers.
  • Zone R30. Herero — Kunene, Omaheke, Otjozondjupa in Namibia; Ghanzi in Botswana. 0,27 million speakers.
  • Zone S10. Shona — Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Namibia, Botswana. 10,8 million speakers.
  • Zone S30. Lozi — Zambia, Zimbabwe, Namibia, Botswana, South Africa. 0,723 million speakers.
  • Zone S30. Tswana — Botswana; North West in South Africa. 4,5 million speakers.
  • Zone S30. Sotho (N) — Gauteng, Limpopo, Mpumalanga in South Africa. 4,1 million speakers.
  • Zone S30. Sotho (S) — Lesotho; Free State in South Africa. 5 million speakers.
  • Zone S40. Zulu — KwaZulu-Natal in South Africa. 26,4 million speakers.
  • Zone S40. Xhosa — Eastern Cape, Western Cape in South Africa. 7,8 million speakers.
  • Zone S40. Swazi — Swaziland; Mpumalanga in South Africa. 2 million speakers.
  • Zone S50. Tsonga — Limpopo, Mpumalanga in South Africa; Maputo, Gaza in Mozambique. 3,7 million speakers.
